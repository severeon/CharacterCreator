# SAVAGE SPECIES

*3.0 to 3.5 Conversion Annotation Guide*

*Keep this document open beside your Savage Species book. Each entry
tells you exactly what to change as you read.*

## How to Use This Document

> **\[KEEP\]** No change needed. This rule, table, or ability works as
> written in 3.5.
>
> **\[CHANGE\]** Must be updated. Replace or modify this content before
> using it in a 3.5 game.
>
> **\[REVIEW\]** Check this against the 3.5 Monster Manual or PHB. May
> need changes depending on the specific creature or DM ruling.

Page number ranges are approximate --- exact pages vary between
printings of Savage Species. Use chapter headings and section titles to
locate content in your copy.

## CHAPTER 1: Introduction and Overview (approx. pp. 4--12)

### General Introduction

> **\[KEEP\]** The core concept of monster characters and ECL is
> compatible with 3.5.
>
> **\[KEEP\]** The Step 0 (\'Check with your DM\') guidance is still
> good advice.
>
> **\[CHANGE\]** Any reference to the \'Shapechanger\' creature type:
> Shapechanger is now a subtype in 3.5, not a type. Assign an
> appropriate primary type to any Shapechanger creature.
>
> **\[CHANGE\]** CR-to-ECL equivalency: The book occasionally implies CR
> is related to ECL. In 3.5, CR and ECL are entirely separate. Ignore
> any such comparisons and use ECL = RHD + LA + class levels.

**The Monster Character Overview Table / ECL Listings**

> **\[REVIEW\]** Cross-check every monster\'s listed LA against the 3.5
> Monster Manual. Some LAs changed or were removed. The 3.5 MM entry is
> authoritative.
>
> **\[CHANGE\]** Any monster listed with LA that no longer has an LA in
> the 3.5 MM (such as the Ghaele) should be treated as not suitable for
> standard PC play without DM approval.

**CHAPTER 2: Building Monster Characters (approx. pp. 13--50)**

**Ability Scores**

> **\[KEEP\]** Both ability score generation methods (point buy + racial
> modifiers, or elite array from monster stats) work in 3.5.
>
> **\[CHANGE\]** Use the 3.5 MM stat block as the endpoint for the
> monster\'s ability scores, not the 3.0 entry. Some monsters gained or
> lost ability score points between editions.
>
> **\[KEEP\]** The rule that monster PCs gain +1 to an ability score
> every 4 character levels is intentional and still applies in 3.5.

**Racial Hit Dice**

> **\[KEEP\]** Racial HD function identically in 3.5. HP, BAB, saves,
> and skills derived from RHD all work the same way.
>
> **\[REVIEW\]** BAB progression by type: Verify the creature type\'s
> BAB rate against the 3.5 rules. Outsiders and Magical Beasts = full
> BAB. Humanoids and Aberrations = 3/4 BAB. Fey and Undead = 1/2 BAB.

**Level Adjustment**

> **\[KEEP\]** The LA framework and the \'acid test\' methodology are
> both compatible with 3.5.
>
> **\[CHANGE\]** DR value --- if used in the LA calculation: DR is less
> powerful in 3.5 (lower numbers, specific bypass types). This may
> justify reducing LA by 1 for creatures whose primary defensive power
> was high DR.
>
> **\[REVIEW\]** Re-evaluate LA for any creature whose key abilities
> changed substantially in 3.5 (see Chapter 12 annotations below).

**Skill Points and Class Skills**

> **\[CHANGE\]** Replace ALL 3.0 skill names with 3.5 equivalents:
> Alchemy -\> Craft (alchemy); Animal Empathy -\> Handle Animal;
> Innuendo -\> Bluff/Sense Motive; Intuit Direction -\> Survival; Read
> Lips -\> Spot; Scry -\> Spellcraft; Wilderness Lore -\> Survival; Pick
> Pocket -\> Sleight of Hand.
>
> **\[KEEP\]** Skill point allocation rates by creature type are the
> same in 3.5.
>
> **\[KEEP\]** The rule that first-level skill points are multiplied by
> 4 applies identically.

**Feats**

> **\[CHANGE\]** Ambidexterity: This feat does not exist in 3.5. Any
> monster that had it should instead be given Two-Weapon Fighting (which
> covers both feats\' effects in 3.5).
>
> **\[CHANGE\]** Expertise: Renamed to Combat Expertise. Same function.
>
> **\[CHANGE\]** Power Attack: Now trades 1-for-1 on one-handed attacks
> and 1-for-2 on two-handed attacks. No longer adds to TWF attacks.
>
> **\[CHANGE\]** Weapon Finesse: Now applies to all light weapons
> generally, not one specific weapon. No longer needs to be taken
> separately per weapon.
>
> **\[KEEP\]** All other feats not listed in the changes section
> function identically or with only minor wording differences.

**Natural Attacks**

> **\[KEEP\]** Natural attack mechanics are essentially the same in 3.5.
>
> **\[CHANGE\]** New 3.5 rule: A creature\'s natural weapons are treated
> as magic weapons for the purpose of overcoming DR once the creature
> has 5 or more Hit Dice. Add this note to any monster class that
> reaches 5 HD during progression.
>
> **\[KEEP\]** Primary natural attacks at full BAB; secondary at BAB -5.
> Manufactured weapon + natural attacks: secondary naturals at -5.

**Damage Reduction --- Building Blocks**

> **\[CHANGE\]** ANY DR of the form DR X/+Y must be converted. Use the
> DR conversion table (Section 3 of the Rules Reference). Reduce the
> numeric value and assign the appropriate bypass type for the creature
> category.
>
> **\[CHANGE\]** \'Half damage from weapons\' or \'half damage from
> non-magical weapons\' mechanics: Convert to flat DR 5/magic or DR
> 10/magic as appropriate.
>
> **\[CHANGE\]** Electricity subtype: Eliminated. Replace with Immunity
> to Electricity in Special Qualities.

**Energy Resistance --- Building Blocks**

> **\[CHANGE\]** All energy resistance values in this chapter are
> per-round values. In 3.5, energy resistance is per hit. Use the
> conversion guidelines: roughly reduce per-round values by 25-35% for
> equivalency against multi-hit sources, or keep them the same for
> equivalency against single-hit sources.

**CHAPTER 3: Monster Classes (approx. pp. 51--180)**

This is the largest chapter and the one you will reference most often.
The annotations below cover universal rules first, then specific
monsters alphabetically.

**Universal Monster Class Rules**

> **\[CHANGE\]** Multiclassing restriction: Savage Species requires
> completing a monster class before taking any other class. Many DMs
> house-rule this away in 3.5 (and the Savage Progressions web articles
> explicitly allow free multiclassing). Discuss with your DM.
>
> **\[KEEP\]** HD, BAB, saves, skill points, and feat progression within
> monster class tables are all compatible with 3.5.
>
> **\[KEEP\]** The monster class must be completed in order (Level 1
> before Level 2, etc.), even if DM allows non-consecutive levels.
>
> **\[CHANGE\]** All DR entries in monster class tables: Convert from
> 3.0 format to 3.5 format (see Rules Reference Section 3).
>
> **\[CHANGE\]** All energy resistance entries: Convert from per-round
> to per-hit (see Rules Reference Section 4).
>
> **\[CHANGE\]** All skill references: Use 3.5 skill names (see Rules
> Reference Section 5).
>
> **\[CHANGE\]** All feat references: Use 3.5 feat names/rules (see
> Rules Reference Section 6).
>
> **\[CHANGE\]** Any SLA referencing Haste: Use 3.5 Haste (group
> speed/attack buff, not action doubler).
>
> **\[CHANGE\]** Any SLA referencing Polymorph Self: Use 3.5 Polymorph.
>
> **\[CHANGE\]** Any SLA referencing Polymorph Other: Use 3.5 Baleful
> Polymorph.
>
> **\[CHANGE\]** Any SLA referencing stat buffs (Bull\'s Strength etc.):
> Fixed +4 bonus, not variable 1d4+1.
