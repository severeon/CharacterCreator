# SAVAGE SPECIES

*Monster Class Conversion Sheets*

3.5 Edition --- Fan Conversion Reference

*Each sheet gives you a ready-to-use 3.5-compatible monster class
progression. All DR, skills, feats, and abilities have been updated.*

## Legend for All Sheets

- ECL = Effective Character Level (RHD + LA + Class Levels)

- RHD = Racial Hit Dice

- LA = Level Adjustment

- BAB = Base Attack Bonus

- Fort / Ref / Will = Base saving throw bonuses from monster class
  levels only

- SLA = Spell-Like Ability. Save DCs for SLAs = 10 + spell level + Cha
  modifier unless noted.

- Natural weapons are treated as magic for overcoming DR when the
  creature has 5+ total HD.

- Ability score increases: +1 to any score at every 4th character level
  (ECL 4, 8, 12, 16, 20).

#### Change Shape (Su)

A doppelganger can assume the appearance of any Small or Medium
humanoid. This functions like the Disguise skill, with a +10 bonus,
except that it changes actual physical form. The doppelganger retains
its own statistics. It can hold a shape indefinitely. Detecting the
change requires a Spot or Sense Motive check opposed by Disguise
(doppelganger\'s check = 1d20 + Disguise ranks + 10 bonus). Use of this
ability is a standard action.

#### Detect Thoughts (Sp)

At will, caster level equals the doppelganger\'s HD. This functions as
the 3.5 spell Detect Thoughts. Will save DC = 10 + spell level (2) + Cha
modifier. The doppelganger is always considered to be actively
concentrating.

#### Immunities

Immune to charm and sleep effects. As a Monstrous Humanoid, the
doppelganger has darkvision 60 ft. and is not subject to the immunities
of other types.

#### Scent (Ex)

The gnoll can detect opponents within 30 ft. by smell. If the opponent
is upwind, range increases to 60 ft.; downwind, 15 ft. The gnoll can
detect a creature\'s direction but not exact location. It can track by

and medium armor.

#### Freeze (Ex)

A gargoyle can hold itself perfectly still, appearing to be a stone
statue. A creature that studies the gargoyle can determine that it is

and ranged weapons from non-magical sources are reduced.

#### Alternate Form (Su)

A werewolf can shift between humanoid, wolf, and hybrid forms as a
standard action. Returning to humanoid form is also a standard action.
Equipment does not change form. In wolf form the character cannot use
hands or speak; in hybrid form hands and speech are available. Assume
wolf form ability scores as noted above relative to humanoid form.

#### Curse of Lycanthropy (Su)

A humanoid bitten by a werewolf in hybrid form must make a DC 15
Fortitude save or become afflicted with lycanthropy. Only a remove
disease or remove curse spell cast within 3 days prevents the
transformation.

#### Control Shape

Natural werewolves are born with the ability to control their
transformation. The Control Shape skill (Wis-based, trained only) is
used during a full moon or when injured below half HP. A natural
werewolf has a starting DC of 15 to control transformation
involuntarily; each monster class level reduces this by 2. By ECL 3,
most involuntary triggers are manageable.

#### Damage Reduction

DR 5/silver. Only silver weapons, or natural weapons or attacks from
creatures with 5+ HD, bypass this DR.

#### Regeneration (Ex)

Fire and acid deal normal damage to a troll. All other damage heals at a
rate of 5 HP per round. A troll that loses a limb or body part can
reattach it by holding the severed member to the stump; it reattaches in
1 minute. Without the member, it regrows in 3d6 minutes. Killing a troll
requires burning it (fire damage to reduce to 0 HP after denying
regeneration) or dealing acid damage to 0 HP.

#### Rend (Ex)

If the troll hits the same opponent with both claw attacks in the same
round, it latches on and tears the flesh. This deals an additional 2d6 +
1.5x Str modifier points of damage automatically (no additional attack
roll needed).

#### Rock Throwing (Ex)

An ogre can hurl rocks weighing 40--50 pounds as a ranged attack with a
range increment of 140 ft. (maximum 700 ft.). Damage is 2d6 + Str
modifier. The ogre must use the rock throwing feat (which is treated as
a racial ability here) to do this without taking nonproficiency
penalties.

#### Armor and Speed

An unarmored ogre moves at 40 ft. When wearing any armor, speed drops to
30 ft. This is already factored into the base statistics.

#### Petrifying Gaze (Su)

Turn to stone permanently, range 30 ft. Fortitude save DC = 15 + Cha
modifier to resist. This is a supernatural gaze attack. Opponents who
wish to avoid the gaze may avert their eyes (-50% chance of being
subject to the gaze each round) or wear a blindfold (total immunity but
the medusa is treated as having total concealment vs. that opponent).

#### Snake Poison (Ex)

Delivered by bite from snake hair. Fortitude save (DC increases as noted
in table). Initial and secondary damage: Str 1d4 (improving to Str 1d6
at ECL 5). The medusa must hit with the snake hair attack to deliver
poison.

#### Avoidance (Ex)

A medusa is immune to its own gaze attack. It cannot be turned to stone
by mirrors, reflections, or its own reflected gaze.

#### Captivating Song (Su)

The harpy\'s song is an enchantment (compulsion) mind-affecting ability.
Any creature within 300 ft. that can hear the song must make a Will save
(DC = 10 + 1/2 harpy\'s HD + Cha modifier) or be captivated. A
captivated victim walks toward the harpy (spending a move action each
round), ignoring other threats. A captivated creature that is blocked
cannot take any action. The effect ends if the harpy stops singing or
the creature moves out of range. Captivated victims receive a new save
each round if harmed. This is a standard action to begin; the harpy may
take other actions while singing.

At ECL 3, only the fascinate effect (victim stops and stares) is
available. At ECL 5, the compulsion (victim moves toward harpy)
activates.
