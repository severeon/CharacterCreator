## Size Categories

D&D 3.5e defines nine size categories for creatures, from Fine (tiny) to Colossal (huge). Size affects several combat statistics.

| Size | Space | Reach | AC Mod | Attack Mod | Hide Mod |
|------|-------|-------|--------|------------|----------|
| Fine | 1/2 ft | 0 ft | +8 | +8 | +16 |
| Diminutive | 1 ft | 0 ft | +4 | +4 | +12 |
| Tiny | 2-1/2 ft | 0 ft | +2 | +2 | +8 |
| Small | 5 ft | 5 ft | +1 | +1 | +4 |
| Medium | 5 ft | 5 ft | +0 | +0 | +0 |
| Large | 10 ft | 10 ft | -1 | -1 | -4 |
| Huge | 15 ft | 15 ft | -2 | -2 | -8 |
| Gargantuan | 20 ft | 20 ft | -4 | -4 | -12 |
| Colossal | 30 ft | 30 ft | -8 | -8 | -16 |

### Size Modifiers

#### Armor Class (AC)
Larger creatures are easier to hit, while smaller creatures are harder to hit.

#### Attack Modifier
Larger creatures have easier time hitting smaller creatures; smaller creatures have harder time hitting larger creatures.

#### Hide Modifier
Smaller creatures can hide more easily; larger creatures are easier to miss.

### Space

The space a creature occupies on the battlefield:

- **Fine through Medium:** 5 feet or less
- **Large:** 10 feet
- **Huge:** 15 feet
- **Gargantuan:** 20 feet
- **Colossal:** 30 feet

### Weapon Size and Damage

Weapons also have sizes. A creature using a weapon larger than its own size deals increased damage, while weapons smaller than the creature's size deal less damage.

| Weapon Size vs. Creature | Damage Modifier |
|--------------------------|-----------------|
| One size category larger | +1 step (2d6 becomes 3d6) |
| Two size categories larger | +2 steps |
| Same size | Normal |
| One size category smaller | -1 step |
| Two size categories smaller | -2 steps |
